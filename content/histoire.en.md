In 1972, a handful of volleyball enthusiasts decided to gather and found a club dedicated to this dynamic and rapidly
growing sport. Motivated by their shared passion and the desire to promote volleyball in their community, these founders
laid the groundwork for what would become a local sporting institution. At that time, volleyball was beginning to gain
popularity in France, and the creation of this club answered a growing need for organized structures to support the
sport.

The club was officially created as an association under the French 1901 law, a legal status that allows groups of people
to associate freely, without profit motive, to pursue a common goal. This legal framework provided the flexibility
needed to organize sporting activities while benefiting from official recognition. The association’s statutes were
carefully drafted, defining the club’s objectives, operating mode, and governance rules. These statutes allowed the
association to be structured and gave it a solid foundation for future development.

The early years of the club were marked by a pioneering spirit and strong cohesion among its members. Trainings often
took place in modest conditions, but the enthusiasm and determination of the players more than compensated for these
constraints. Quickly, the club began to organise friendly matches and local tournaments, attracting new members and
strengthening its reputation. These events also helped forge close ties with other clubs and sports associations in the
region.

Over the years, the club has evolved and adapted to changes, both sporting and administrative. The commitment of
volunteers has played a crucial role in this dynamic, enabling the club to offer varied and quality activities. Youth
sections were created to introduce the youngest to the joys of volleyball, while senior teams continued to represent the
club in increasingly demanding competitions. This diversification has helped make the club an essential player in the
local sporting landscape.

In the last ten years, the club has grown significantly, becoming one of the largest volleyball clubs in France. Today,
it proudly ranks 38th nationally in terms of membership, a testament to its attractiveness and dynamism. This notable
growth results from hard work and a clear vision from all club members, who have managed to attract new talent and
retain existing players.

Furthermore, the club has achieved remarkable sporting progress, moving from a departmental level to a regional level
over the past three years. This ascent is the result of an ambitious strategy aimed at improving team performance and
offering higher‑level competition opportunities. Players, coaches, and volunteers have all contributed to this success,
working together to achieve common goals and raise the club’s level of play.

Today, the volleyball club founded in 1972 can boast a rich past and a dynamic present. It remains faithful to the
values that guided its creation: passion for the sport, team spirit, and associative commitment. Thanks to the continued
involvement of its members and the warm welcome given to newcomers, the club continues to thrive and convey its love of
volleyball to future generations. This history is a testimony to how a simple idea, carried by passionate people, can
become a lasting and enriching collective adventure.

The club looks to the future with optimism and ambition. Projects are abundant, whether it concerns infrastructure
development, training of young talent, or organizing large‑scale sporting events. The club is determined to continue its
progress and establish itself as a reference for volleyball in France. With an ever‑growing and engaged community, the
club is ready to meet upcoming challenges and write new chapters in its history.
